Charlie's Cafe


Durian lovers rejoice! The new D24 durian soft serve has finally landed in Charlie's Cafe. This pay-it-forward cafe has been one of the most popular places to enjoy your daily fix of Malaysian cuisine. From nasi lemak to curry mee, they have it all. Now, you can finish your meal with a smooth, rich and delicate cup/cone of D24 durian soft serve! Made with fresh durian plumps, ensuring maximum flavour. It also has a nice balance of sweetness, so do not worry so much about that unwanted sugar rush. All in all, Charlie's Cafe's durian softserve is certainly one of the best out there.

29, Jalan Bukit Desa 5, Taman Bukit Desa, 58100, Taman Desa, Kuala Lumpur

Inside Scoop


Any KL-rians would be familiar with the name Inside Scoop that has built its name to be one of the best ice cream parlours around Kuala Lumpur and Klang Valley. With Inside Scoop's flavourful arrays of ice cream to choose from, this name is an absolute gem for dessert's lovers. Ranging from the classic vanilla beans to much more exotic ones such as the best-selling cempedak flavour, durian - King of all fruits - and teh tarik flavour. The locality of the flavour really bursts through to your tongue with cempedak bits hinting every scoop you are taking, and richness of the teh tarik is strong to strike on your palette.

llaollao


Ilao Ilao has been incredibly well received overseas, especially in Europe and Singapore. Fortunately, we do not have to travel all the way there for natural frozen yoghurt as Ilao Ilao was brought into Malaysia recently. They offer amazingly good yoghurt with a series of toppings, fresh fruits and homemade sauce. Fruits are cut daily and fresh berries (not frozen) are imported. Yoghurt is made from skimmed milk and has a low-fat content, which makes it melt a little quicker, but the flavours are absolutely to die for!

Booza Turkish Ice Cream


Nestled within the bustling Bukit Bintang, Booza has been making waves with their homemade Turkish ice cream. If you’re active on social media, you may have stumbled across ice cream carts in Turkey where the ice cream master often pulls out tricks with long scoops and cones. And expect the same here with a range of flavours that come in thick, gooey and chewy texture. It might sound like a different league of its own but chomping down this refreshing ice cream will have your appreciating the scorching sun.
